Magnesium is one of the most common supplements people reach for when they want
to sleep better — but "magnesium" isn't one thing. The form matters for how well
it's absorbed and how it affects digestion.

## The two forms at a glance

**Magnesium glycinate** is magnesium bound to the amino acid glycine. It's often
chosen because it tends to be gentle on the stomach and is well absorbed.

**Magnesium citrate** is magnesium bound to citric acid. It's well absorbed too,
but it has a mild laxative effect, which is why it's sometimes used for occasional
constipation rather than sleep.

## What the research actually shows

Evidence that magnesium supplementation improves sleep is limited and mixed. It
may support relaxation in people who are genuinely low in magnesium, but the
effect in well-nourished people appears small. [^1]

Magnesium is involved in hundreds of processes in the body, including muscle and
nerve function. [^2] That broad role is often over-extrapolated into strong sleep
claims that the data doesn't fully support.

## So which should you pick?

- Prone to loose stools, or just want the gentler option for nighttime? Many
  people prefer **glycinate**.
- Also dealing with occasional constipation? **Citrate** does double duty.

Neither is a sleep aid in the medical sense. If you have ongoing sleep problems,
that's a conversation for your clinician.
