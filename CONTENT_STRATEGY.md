# Healthy Logs — Content Strategy (for the owner)

> The site is the vehicle; content is the fuel. This is how you avoid building a beautiful site
> nobody finds. Not code — strategy. Hand the relevant bits to writers (or to Claude when
> drafting articles).

## The core problem

Commercial keywords ("best protein powder") are saturated and dominated by huge sites with
years of authority. A brand-new domain cannot rank for those on day one. Google's Helpful
Content / core updates have also wiped out thin affiliate sites. So:

**Strategy: earn topical authority with helpful informational content first, then monetize with
commercial pages once you've earned trust.**

## Content mix

- **70% informational** (early on): "Does magnesium help with sleep?", "Signs of low protein
  intake", "Is oat milk healthy?". These rank for long-tail questions, build authority, and
  capture people early in their journey. Low competition, real traffic.
- **30% commercial** (grows over time): "Best magnesium supplements for sleep", "Top electrolyte
  powders compared". These carry the affiliate links and convert.
- **Interlink them.** Informational articles link to the commercial roundup; the roundup links
  back to the explainers. This forms topical clusters Google rewards.

## Topical clusters (pick 2–3 to start, go deep, don't go wide)

Example cluster: **Magnesium**
- Pillar: "The Complete Guide to Magnesium" (informational, long)
- Spokes: glycinate vs citrate, magnesium for sleep, magnesium for cramps, signs of deficiency,
  magnesium and anxiety (compliant phrasing!), dosage and safety
- Commercial: "Best magnesium supplements [year]", "Best magnesium for sleep"

Repeat for a couple more clusters (e.g., protein, gut health). Owning a cluster beats scattering
one-off posts across 20 topics.

## Roundup methodology (state it — it's an E-E-A-T and FTC thing)

Every "best X" article should include a short "How we chose" box: criteria like third-party
testing, bioavailability/form, dosage, value, brand transparency. This makes the ranking
defensible, builds trust, and protects you from "deceptive" claims.

## Compliance in writing (memorize this)

For supplements/foods, NEVER claim a product treats, cures, or prevents disease. Use:
- ✅ "may support", "is associated with", "research suggests", "may help maintain"
- ❌ "cures anxiety", "treats insomnia", "prevents heart disease"

Cite a real source after health claims. This is both legal protection and ranking fuel.

## Cadence

Consistency beats bursts. Even 2 well-researched, reviewed articles per week compounds. Update
old articles (the "log/UPD" model) — refreshing content is one of the highest-ROI SEO moves and
fits the brand.

## Distribution (don't just publish and pray)

- **Google** is the long game (3–9 months to see real organic traction on a new domain — be
  patient).
- **Pinterest** converts well for nutrition/wellness — create pins for articles.
- **A newsletter** (Beehiiv/ConvertKit) builds an audience you own, independent of Google's
  algorithm. Add the signup from day one even if you email rarely.
- Short-form (Reels/Shorts/TikTok) can drive discovery, repurposing article points.

## Measuring what matters

- **Google Search Console** — impressions, clicks, which queries you rank for. Your compass.
- **GA4** (consent-gated) — traffic, behavior.
- **Affiliate dashboards** — clicks vs. conversions per article. Double down on what earns.

## A realistic timeline

- Months 0–3: build site, publish 20–40 foundational articles, get indexed. ~Little revenue.
- Months 3–6: organic traffic starts; first affiliate sales; apply to brand-direct programs.
- Months 6–12: clusters mature, traffic compounds, add display ads if eligible.
- Year 2+: this is when content sites typically become meaningfully profitable.

Affiliate content is a compounding asset, not a quick flip. The build is the easy 10%; the
consistent, trustworthy content is the 90%.
